4Many readers would no doubt take exception to referring to birds, fish, or even social insects as ‘simple automata’. That is not my intent, as it is absolutely clear that any of these creatures is capable of displaying complex behaviour on its own. The point is that complex individual behaviour is not absolutely required for complex social behaviour to emerge. Even automata obeying simple rules can result in complex, intelligent collective behaviour, as long as sufficient interactions exist that help to reinforce good solutions and explore nearby parts of the space preferentially.
